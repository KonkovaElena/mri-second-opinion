# Retrospective Reader-Study Protocol

Date: 2026-03-30

## Purpose

This document defines the reader-study protocol for evaluating the brain volumetry workflow against expert neuroradiologist interpretation.

It describes the study design that should be executed before the repository can make performance claims beyond its current research-use-only posture.

## Study Objective

To measure the concordance between automated volumetric findings generated by the MRI second-opinion workflow and independent expert neuroradiologist reads, for the current neuro-first workflow family.

## Study Design

### Design Type

Retrospective multi-reader study with blinded comparison.

### Population

Cases should be drawn from publicly available or appropriately governed structural MRI datasets that include T1-weighted sequences suitable for brain volumetry.

Candidate data sources include OASIS-3 and IXI, subject to their respective access terms, cohort properties, and usage restrictions.

### Inclusion Criteria

1. T1-weighted 3D structural MRI with isotropic or near-isotropic resolution
2. complete acquisition without gross motion artifact as assessed by automated QC
3. adult subjects aged 18 years and older
4. study metadata sufficient to reconstruct scanner vendor, field strength, and basic protocol parameters

### Exclusion Criteria

1. studies with incomplete acquisitions or missing critical sequences
2. studies flagged as QC-reject by the automated quality gate
3. pediatric subjects where normative volumetric reference ranges are not established
4. studies with known surgical resections or gross structural abnormalities that invalidate volumetric comparison against normative data

### Sample Size Justification

The minimum sample size should be determined by a pre-study power calculation targeting:

1. primary endpoint: overall concordance between automated and expert volumetric assessments
2. effect size: clinically meaningful agreement threshold
3. power: 0.80 minimum
4. alpha: 0.05 two-sided

A preliminary estimate suggests a minimum of 100 cases for the primary analysis, with at least 20 cases per primary subgroup stratum.

## Readers

### Expert Readers

A minimum of 2 board-certified neuroradiologists should independently assess each case.

Reader qualifications:

1. board certification in radiology with neuroradiology subspecialty training or equivalent experience
2. minimum 3 years post-training clinical practice
3. familiarity with volumetric brain assessment

### Blinding

1. readers are blinded to automated workflow outputs during their assessments
2. readers are blinded to each other's assessments
3. automated workflow runs are blinded to reader assessments

## Endpoints

### Primary Endpoint

Concordance between automated volumetric findings and expert consensus for hippocampal volume and total brain volume, measured by:

1. intraclass correlation coefficient
2. Bland-Altman limits of agreement
3. categorical agreement on clinically relevant thresholds

### Secondary Endpoints

1. per-reader agreement with automated findings
2. inter-reader agreement
3. subgroup-stratified agreement per the bias analysis framework strata
4. automated QC correctly identifying cases that readers would reject

## Assessment Protocol

### For Expert Readers

Each reader independently assesses each case and records:

1. qualitative assessment of brain volume
2. specific regional volume assessments for hippocampus bilaterally
3. presence of clinically relevant atrophy or volume deviation
4. confidence level in the assessment
5. any quality concerns about the input data

### For Automated Workflow

Each case is processed through the standard workflow pipeline and the finalized report is used as the automated assessment.

## Analysis Plan

### Primary Analysis

1. compute ICC between automated measurements and reader consensus
2. construct Bland-Altman plots for each volumetric measurement
3. compute sensitivity and specificity for clinically relevant volume thresholds

### Subgroup Analysis

Per the bias analysis framework, stratify results by:

1. scanner vendor
2. field strength
3. age band
4. acquisition quality class

Report per-stratum metrics and flag any stratum where automated performance degrades materially below the pooled result.

### Missing Data

1. cases with incomplete reader assessments are excluded from per-reader analysis but included in consensus analysis if at least 2 of 3 readers completed the case
2. cases where automated workflow fails or rejects are reported separately as workflow failure rate

## Limitations

This protocol applies to the current neuro-first brain volumetry workflow only.

It does not cover lesion workflows, tumor workflows, or any workflow family not currently implemented.

The protocol is designed for retrospective evaluation and does not claim prospective clinical trial validity.

## Prerequisites

Before executing this protocol:

1. the automated workflow must be at a stable version with all export seams verified
2. the regulatory governance pack must be current
3. data access agreements for the chosen dataset must be in place
4. reader agreements and compensation arrangements must be established
5. IRB or ethics review must be completed if required by the data governance policy
